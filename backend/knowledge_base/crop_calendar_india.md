## Crop Seasons in India

India has three main cropping seasons:

1. **Kharif (Monsoon Season)**: Sown June-July with monsoon onset, harvested September-November
2. **Rabi (Winter Season)**: Sown October-November after monsoon, harvested March-May
3. **Zaid (Summer Season)**: Short season March-June, mainly vegetables and fruits

## Detailed Crop Calendar

### RABI CROPS (Winter — Sown Oct-Nov, Harvested Mar-May)

#### Wheat (Gehun)
- **Type**: Rabi
- **Sowing**: October-November (North India: mid-Oct to end-Nov)
- **Harvesting**: March-April
- **Best Sell Window**: April-May (fresh harvest, good prices if you don't need to sell immediately consider storing 2-3 months for better prices as supply eases)
- **Peak Supply Months**: April-May (prices typically under pressure)
- **Off-Season**: August-September (stored wheat fetches 10-15% premium)
- **Major States**: Punjab, Haryana, UP, MP, Rajasthan
- **Key Varieties**: HD-2967 (Punjab/Haryana), Lokwan (MP/Maharashtra for chapati), Sharbati (MP premium), Mill Quality
- **MSP Procurement**: April-June by FCI
- **Storage**: Can store 6-12 months in dry bags, moisture <12%

#### Gram/Chana (Chickpea)
- **Type**: Rabi
- **Sowing**: October-November
- **Harvesting**: March-April
- **Best Sell Window**: Immediately after harvest or hold 3-4 months (prices rise post-monsoon)
- **Peak Supply**: March-April (fresh arrivals)
- **Off-Season**: September-October (prices typically higher)
- **Major States**: MP, Rajasthan, Maharashtra, UP, Karnataka
- **Key Varieties**: Desi Chana, Kabuli Chana
- **MSP Procurement**: March-June by NAFED

#### Mustard (Sarson)
- **Type**: Rabi
- **Sowing**: October-November
- **Harvesting**: February-March
- **Best Sell Window**: March-April (fresh crop) or hold for June-July (oilseed demand rises)
- **Peak Supply**: February-March
- **Major States**: Rajasthan, Haryana, MP, UP, Gujarat
- **MSP Procurement**: February-May by NAFED

#### Barley (Jau)
- **Type**: Rabi
- **Sowing**: October-November
- **Harvesting**: March-April
- **Best Sell Window**: April-May
- **Major States**: Rajasthan, UP, Haryana, MP

#### Lentil/Masur
- **Type**: Rabi
- **Sowing**: October-November
- **Harvesting**: March-April
- **Best Sell Window**: April or hold for monsoon season (July-Aug prices rise)
- **Major States**: MP, UP, Bihar, West Bengal

#### Coriander (Dhania)
- **Type**: Rabi
- **Sowing**: October-November
- **Harvesting**: February-March
- **Best Sell Window**: March (fresh) or store for 3-6 months for better rates
- **Major States**: Rajasthan, MP, Gujarat, AP

#### Cumin (Jeera)
- **Type**: Rabi
- **Sowing**: October-November
- **Harvesting**: February-March
- **Best Sell Window**: March (fresh arrivals) — Cumin prices highly volatile, traded on NCDEX
- **Major States**: Rajasthan (70% of India's production), Gujarat

### KHARIF CROPS (Monsoon — Sown Jun-Jul, Harvested Sep-Nov)

#### Rice/Paddy (Dhan)
- **Type**: Kharif
- **Sowing**: June-July (transplanting in nurseries starts May)
- **Harvesting**: October-November
- **Best Sell Window**: October-December (MSP procurement active) or store for March-April
- **Peak Supply**: October-November (heavy pressure)
- **Major States**: Punjab, Haryana, UP, West Bengal, AP, Telangana, Tamil Nadu, Bihar
- **Key Varieties**: Basmati (premium), Sona Masoori, IR-36, Ponni
- **MSP Procurement**: October-January by FCI
- **Storage**: Can store 6-12 months, moisture must be <14%

#### Soyabean
- **Type**: Kharif
- **Sowing**: June-July
- **Harvesting**: October-November
- **Best Sell Window**: November-December (fresh) or hold 2-3 months for better rates
- **Peak Supply**: October-November
- **Off-Season**: April-May (limited stocks, higher prices)
- **Major States**: MP (60% of India's production), Maharashtra, Rajasthan
- **MSP Procurement**: October-January by NAFED

#### Maize (Makka)
- **Type**: Kharif
- **Sowing**: June-July
- **Harvesting**: September-October
- **Best Sell Window**: October (immediate) or hold for poultry feed demand peak in winter
- **Major States**: Karnataka, MP, Bihar, Rajasthan, Maharashtra

#### Cotton (Kapas)
- **Type**: Kharif
- **Sowing**: May-June
- **Harvesting**: October-November-December (multiple pickings)
- **Best Sell Window**: December-January (ginning mills active, good demand)
- **Peak Supply**: October-December
- **Major States**: Gujarat, Maharashtra, Telangana, AP, Haryana, Rajasthan, MP
- **MSP Procurement**: October-March by CCI

#### Bajra (Pearl Millet)
- **Type**: Kharif
- **Sowing**: June-July
- **Harvesting**: September-October
- **Best Sell Window**: October (fresh) or hold for winter demand
- **Major States**: Rajasthan, Haryana, Gujarat, UP, Maharashtra

#### Jowar (Sorghum)
- **Type**: Kharif
- **Sowing**: June-July
- **Harvesting**: October-November
- **Best Sell Window**: November-December
- **Major States**: Maharashtra, Karnataka, Rajasthan, MP

#### Moong (Green Gram)
- **Type**: Kharif
- **Sowing**: June-July
- **Harvesting**: September-October
- **Best Sell Window**: September-October (fresh) or hold for festival season demand (Diwali/winter)
- **Major States**: Rajasthan, MP, Maharashtra, Karnataka

#### Urad (Black Gram)
- **Type**: Kharif
- **Sowing**: June-July
- **Harvesting**: September-October
- **Best Sell Window**: October (fresh arrivals) — demand rises in winter
- **Major States**: MP, UP, Rajasthan, Maharashtra, AP

#### Groundnut (Moongfali)
- **Type**: Kharif
- **Sowing**: June-July
- **Harvesting**: October-November
- **Best Sell Window**: November-December (fresh) or hold for oil demand in summer
- **Major States**: Gujarat (50% of India), Rajasthan, AP, Tamil Nadu, Karnataka

### YEAR-ROUND / MULTIPLE SEASON CROPS

#### Onion (Pyaaz)
- **Type**: Multiple seasons (Kharif, Late Kharif, Rabi)
- **Kharif Sowing**: June-August → Harvest: October-December
- **Rabi Sowing**: December-January → Harvest: April-May (biggest crop)
- **Late Kharif**: August-September → Harvest: January-February
- **Best Sell Window**: September-October (between seasons, prices peak — low supply)
- **Price Pattern**: Prices typically lowest April-May (Rabi harvest glut), highest August-October
- **Major States**: Maharashtra (Nashik/Pune), MP, Karnataka, Gujarat, Rajasthan
- **Storage**: Can store 3-4 months in ventilated structures

#### Tomato (Tamatar)
- **Type**: Year-round
- **Multiple Sowings**: June-August and September-October
- **Harvests**: October-March (winter crop), May-July (summer)
- **Best Sell Window**: Sell quickly — highly perishable (5-7 days ambient)
- **Price Pattern**: Prices very volatile. Typically low Dec-Feb (peak supply), high June-August
- **Major States**: MP, Karnataka, AP, Maharashtra, West Bengal

#### Potato (Aloo)
- **Type**: Rabi
- **Sowing**: October-November
- **Harvesting**: January-March
- **Best Sell Window**: March (fresh) or cold-store for June-September (cold stored potatoes get 30-50% premium)
- **Price Pattern**: Lowest Feb-March (harvest glut), highest July-September
- **Major States**: UP (40% of India), West Bengal, Bihar, Gujarat, MP
- **Storage**: 4-8 months in cold storage at 4-10°C

#### Garlic (Lehsun)
- **Type**: Rabi
- **Sowing**: September-November
- **Harvesting**: February-April
- **Best Sell Window**: March (fresh) or dry and store 3-6 months
- **Major States**: MP, Rajasthan, Gujarat, UP

#### Turmeric (Haldi)
- **Type**: Kharif (long duration — 8-9 months)
- **Sowing**: June-July
- **Harvesting**: January-March
- **Best Sell Window**: After proper drying and polishing (adds 20-30% value)
- **Major States**: Maharashtra, Telangana, Tamil Nadu, AP, Karnataka

#### Red Chilli (Lal Mirch)
- **Type**: Kharif
- **Sowing**: July-August
- **Harvesting**: December-February
- **Best Sell Window**: February-March (dried) or hold for off-season premium
- **Major States**: AP, Telangana, Karnataka, MP, Maharashtra

## General Selling Tips by Season

### During Harvest Season (Peak Supply):
- Prices are typically LOWEST during harvest as supply floods mandis
- If you can afford to store, hold for 2-3 months post-harvest
- MSP procurement is active — sell to government agencies if mandi price < MSP

### During Off-Season (2-4 months after harvest):
- Prices typically 10-25% HIGHER than harvest time
- Storage costs eat into profit — calculate break-even
- Best for non-perishable crops (Wheat, Chana, Mustard, Soyabean)

### Festival Seasons (Diwali, Wedding Season):
- Pulses and spices see demand surge
- Onion and Potato prices often spike
- Good time to sell stored produce
