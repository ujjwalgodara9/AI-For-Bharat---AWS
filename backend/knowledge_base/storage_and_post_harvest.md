# Post-Harvest Storage Guide for Indian Farmers
## Crop-wise Storage Methods, Shelf Life, and Warehouse Guidance
## Source: ICAR, FCI Guidelines, Warehousing Corporation Standards
## Last Updated: March 2026

---

## Why Storage Matters

Post-harvest losses in India are estimated at 5-15% for grains and 25-40% for fruits and vegetables. Proper storage can:
- Save your crop from spoilage, insects, and moisture damage
- Allow you to sell later at higher prices (off-season premium)
- Enable you to meet FAQ (Fair Average Quality) standards for MSP procurement

---

## Storage Categories

### Category 1: Long-Term Storable (6-12+ months)
These crops can be stored for months with minimal loss if conditions are right.

### Category 2: Medium-Term Storable (2-6 months)
Require careful moisture control and pest management.

### Category 3: Perishable (Days to weeks)
Must be sold quickly or require cold storage infrastructure.

## Detailed Storage Guide by Crop

### Wheat (Gehun) — Long-Term Storable
- **Shelf Life**: 6-12 months (up to 2 years in proper warehouse)
- **Perishability**: Very Low (1/10)
- **Moisture Requirement**: Must be below 12% before storage
- **Method**: Store in clean, dry gunny/jute bags. Place bags on wooden pallets (not directly on floor). Use neem leaves or neem oil between layers to repel insects.
- **Temperature**: 25-30°C (room temperature is fine)
- **Humidity**: Must be below 60%
- **Warehouse Eligible**: YES — FCI and CWC warehouses accept wheat
- **Common Problems**: Weevils (ghun), moisture damage, fungal growth
- **Prevention**: Sun-dry grain for 2-3 days before storage. Check every 15 days. Fumigate if needed.
- **Storage Cost**: Approximately Rs 1.5/quintal/day in private warehouse
- **Hindi**: सूखी बोरियों में हवादार कमरे में रखें, नमी 12% से कम रखें। कीड़ों से बचाव के लिए नीम की पत्तियाँ रखें।

### Rice/Paddy (Dhan) — Long-Term Storable
- **Shelf Life**: 6-12 months (paddy stores better than milled rice)
- **Perishability**: Very Low (1/10)
- **Moisture Requirement**: Below 14% for paddy, below 12% for milled rice
- **Method**: Store in dry jute bags on wooden pallets. Ensure air circulation. Paddy with husk stores better than de-husked rice.
- **Temperature**: 25-30°C
- **Humidity**: Below 65%
- **Warehouse Eligible**: YES — FCI and state agencies accept paddy
- **Common Problems**: Grain borers, moisture re-absorption in monsoon
- **Prevention**: Keep away from walls. Stack max 10 bags high. Check moisture monthly.
- **Storage Cost**: Approximately Rs 1.5/quintal/day
- **Hindi**: सूखी जूट की बोरियों में, लकड़ी के फट्टों पर रखें। नमी 14% से कम रखें।

### Soyabean — Medium-Term Storable
- **Shelf Life**: 3-6 months
- **Perishability**: Low (2/10)
- **Moisture Requirement**: Below 10% (critical — soyabean absorbs moisture easily)
- **Method**: Clean dry bags, store in cool dry place away from moisture. Airtight containers preferred.
- **Temperature**: 20-25°C (cooler is better)
- **Humidity**: Below 65%
- **Warehouse Eligible**: YES — NAFED procurement centers and CWC warehouses
- **Common Problems**: Oil rancidity if moisture high, fungal growth, discoloration
- **Prevention**: Dry thoroughly before storage. Don't mix old and new stock.
- **Storage Cost**: Approximately Rs 2/quintal/day
- **Hindi**: साफ सूखी बोरियों में, नमी से दूर। ठंडी सूखी जगह पर रखें। नमी 10% से कम रखें।

### Chana/Gram (Chickpea) — Long-Term Storable
- **Shelf Life**: 6-12 months
- **Perishability**: Very Low (1/10)
- **Moisture Requirement**: Below 10%
- **Method**: Dry clean bags, store elevated on pallets. Add dry neem leaves for pest control. Can also mix with dried red chilli powder.
- **Temperature**: 25-30°C
- **Humidity**: Below 60%
- **Warehouse Eligible**: YES — NAFED procurement
- **Common Problems**: Pulse beetle (dal ka keeda), bruchids
- **Prevention**: Sun-dry 3-4 days. Mix with neem leaves or turmeric powder.
- **Hindi**: सूखी साफ बोरियों में ऊंचाई पर रखें। कीट नियंत्रण के लिए सूखी नीम की पत्तियाँ डालें।

### Mustard (Sarson) — Long-Term Storable
- **Shelf Life**: 6-12 months
- **Perishability**: Very Low (1/10)
- **Moisture Requirement**: Below 8%
- **Method**: Airtight containers or clean bags. Keep completely dry and cool. Oil content makes it prone to rancidity if moist.
- **Temperature**: 20-25°C
- **Humidity**: Below 60%
- **Warehouse Eligible**: YES — NAFED procurement
- **Common Problems**: Oil goes rancid if moisture high, mustard aphid
- **Hindi**: एयरटाइट बर्तनों या साफ बोरियों में रखें। सूखा और ठंडा रखें।

### Maize (Makka) — Medium-Term Storable
- **Shelf Life**: 3-6 months (on cob: shorter)
- **Perishability**: Very Low (1/10)
- **Moisture Requirement**: Below 12%
- **Method**: Dry to 12% moisture before storage. Use metal bins if possible (protects from rodents). Can also use improved mud bins (kothi) with proper sealing.
- **Temperature**: 25-30°C
- **Humidity**: Below 65%
- **Warehouse Eligible**: YES
- **Common Problems**: Aflatoxin (if stored moist), weevils, rodents
- **Prevention**: Thorough drying is CRITICAL. Check regularly for hotspots.
- **Hindi**: भंडारण से पहले 12% नमी तक सुखाएं। हो सके तो धातु के डिब्बों में रखें।

### Cotton (Kapas) — Long-Term Storable
- **Shelf Life**: 6-12 months (if dry)
- **Perishability**: Very Low (1/10)
- **Moisture Requirement**: Below 8% (unginned), below 10% (ginned)
- **Method**: Press into bales, store in dry covered area. Avoid direct sunlight. Keep away from oil, chemicals.
- **Temperature**: 25-35°C (not critical)
- **Humidity**: Below 65%
- **Warehouse Eligible**: YES — CCI procurement
- **Hindi**: गांठों में दबाएं, सूखी ढकी जगह में रखें। सीधी धूप से बचाएं।

### Bajra (Pearl Millet) — Long-Term Storable
- **Shelf Life**: 6-12 months
- **Perishability**: Very Low (1/10)
- **Moisture Requirement**: Below 12%
- **Method**: Store in dry airtight containers. Sun-dry thoroughly before storage.
- **Temperature**: 25-30°C
- **Humidity**: Below 60%
- **Warehouse Eligible**: YES
- **Hindi**: सूखे एयरटाइट बर्तनों में रखें। भंडारण से पहले धूप में सुखाएं।

### Jowar (Sorghum) — Long-Term Storable
- **Shelf Life**: 6-12 months
- **Perishability**: Very Low (1/10)
- **Method**: Store in jute bags on raised platforms. Keep well-ventilated.
- **Warehouse Eligible**: YES
- **Hindi**: जूट की बोरियों में ऊंचे चबूतरे पर रखें। हवादार रखें।

### Moong (Green Gram) — Medium-Term Storable
- **Shelf Life**: 3-6 months
- **Perishability**: Low (2/10)
- **Moisture Requirement**: Below 10%
- **Method**: Store in airtight bags with dried neem leaves. Keep completely dry.
- **Warehouse Eligible**: YES — NAFED procurement
- **Common Problems**: Bruchids (pulse beetle) — very common in moong
- **Hindi**: सूखी नीम के साथ एयरटाइट बैग में रखें। सूखा रखें।

### Urad (Black Gram) — Medium-Term Storable
- **Shelf Life**: 3-6 months
- **Perishability**: Low (2/10)
- **Moisture Requirement**: Below 10%
- **Method**: Clean dry airtight containers. Use turmeric powder mixed in for natural pest control.
- **Warehouse Eligible**: YES — NAFED procurement
- **Hindi**: साफ सूखे एयरटाइट बर्तनों में। कीट नियंत्रण के लिए हल्दी पाउडर मिलाएं।

### Groundnut (Moongfali) — Medium-Term Storable
- **Shelf Life**: 3-6 months (in shell), 1-3 months (shelled)
- **Perishability**: Low (2/10)
- **Moisture Requirement**: Below 8% (critical — aflatoxin risk if moist)
- **Method**: Dry to 8% moisture. Store in jute bags in cool ventilated area. In-shell lasts longer.
- **Warehouse Eligible**: YES
- **Common Problems**: Aflatoxin (carcinogenic mold) — major concern
- **Prevention**: NEVER store moist groundnut. Check for mold regularly.
- **Hindi**: 8% नमी तक सुखाएं। ठंडी हवादार जगह में जूट की बोरियों में रखें।

### PERISHABLE CROPS (Sell quickly or cold-store)

### Onion (Pyaaz) — Semi-Perishable
- **Shelf Life**: 3-4 months (ventilated storage), 5-6 months (cold storage)
- **Perishability**: Medium-High (6/10)
- **Method**: Well-ventilated storage with good airflow. Do NOT stack — use bamboo shelves or racks. Remove damaged onions immediately.
- **Temperature**: 25-30°C (ambient ventilated) or 0-2°C (cold storage)
- **Humidity**: Below 70%
- **Warehouse Eligible**: NO for MSP (no MSP). Use NAFED buffer stock schemes.
- **Common Problems**: Sprouting, rotting (black mold), neck rot
- **Prevention**: Cure onions for 10-15 days after harvest before storage. Remove tops.
- **Storage Cost**: Approximately Rs 5/quintal/day
- **Hindi**: अच्छी हवा वाली जगह में, ढेर न लगाएं। बांस की अलमारियों पर रखें।

### Tomato (Tamatar) — Highly Perishable
- **Shelf Life**: 5-7 days (ambient), 2-3 weeks (cold storage at 10-15°C)
- **Perishability**: Very High (9/10)
- **Method**: Cool shade, do NOT stack or press. Sell within 2-3 days. Refrigerate if possible. Pick slightly green for longer shelf life.
- **Temperature**: 10-15°C (cold storage), NOT below 10°C (chilling injury)
- **Humidity**: 85-90%
- **Warehouse Eligible**: NO
- **Recommendation**: SELL AS SOON AS POSSIBLE. Do not wait for price increase.
- **Storage Cost**: Approximately Rs 15/quintal/day (cold storage)
- **Hindi**: ठंडी छाया में रखें, ढेर न लगाएं। 2-3 दिन में बेचें। हो सके तो फ्रिज में रखें।

### Potato (Aloo) — Semi-Perishable
- **Shelf Life**: 2-3 weeks (ambient), 4-8 months (cold storage at 4-10°C)
- **Perishability**: Medium-High (6/10)
- **Method**: Store in dark, cool, ventilated area. Avoid sunlight (causes greening — toxic solanine). Cold storage is the standard practice in India for potatoes.
- **Temperature**: 4-10°C (cold storage ideal)
- **Humidity**: 85-90%
- **Warehouse Eligible**: YES (cold storage)
- **Common Problems**: Greening (sunlight), sprouting, soft rot
- **Prevention**: Keep in total darkness. Remove sprouted/damaged potatoes.
- **Storage Cost**: Approximately Rs 4/quintal/day (cold storage)
- **Hindi**: अंधेरी, ठंडी, हवादार जगह में रखें। धूप से बचाएं (हरा हो जाता है)।

### Garlic (Lehsun) — Medium-Term Storable
- **Shelf Life**: 3-6 months (well-dried)
- **Perishability**: Medium (5/10)
- **Method**: Hang in mesh bags or bamboo baskets with good airflow. Keep completely dry. Curing for 10-15 days after harvest is essential.
- **Temperature**: 25-30°C (ambient) or 0-4°C (cold storage)
- **Humidity**: Below 60%
- **Warehouse Eligible**: NO for MSP (no MSP)
- **Hindi**: जालीदार बैग में लटकाएं, अच्छी हवा दें। सूखा रखें।

### Turmeric (Haldi) — Medium-Term Storable
- **Shelf Life**: 6-12 months (properly dried and polished)
- **Perishability**: Low-Medium (3/10)
- **Method**: Boil, dry thoroughly in sun for 10-15 days, then polish. Store in gunny bags. Well-dried turmeric can last months.
- **Warehouse Eligible**: YES (after drying)
- **Hindi**: अच्छी तरह सुखाएं, बोरियों में रखें। महीनों तक रख सकते हैं।

### Red Chilli (Lal Mirch) — Medium-Term Storable
- **Shelf Life**: 6-12 months (fully dried)
- **Perishability**: Low-Medium (3/10)
- **Method**: Sun-dry fully until moisture is below 10%. Store in airtight bags. Keep away from moisture.
- **Warehouse Eligible**: YES (dried)
- **Hindi**: पूरी तरह धूप में सुखाएं, एयरटाइट बैग में रखें। नमी से दूर रखें।

### Coriander Seeds (Dhania) — Medium-Term Storable
- **Shelf Life**: 6-12 months
- **Perishability**: Low-Medium (3/10)
- **Method**: Dry seeds well. Store in cloth bags in cool area. Avoid plastic bags (cause sweating).
- **Hindi**: बीजों को अच्छी तरह सुखाएं, कपड़े की बोरियों में ठंडी जगह रखें।

### Cumin Seeds (Jeera) — Medium-Term Storable
- **Shelf Life**: 6-12 months
- **Perishability**: Low (2/10)
- **Method**: Sun-dry completely. Store in airtight containers. Very high value — store carefully.
- **Hindi**: पूरी तरह धूप में सुखाएं, एयरटाइट बर्तनों में रखें।

## Weather Impact on Storage

| Weather Condition | Effect on Shelf Life | Risk | Advice |
|-------------------|---------------------|------|--------|
| Heavy Rain/Monsoon | Shelf life HALVED | Moisture damage, fungal growth, faster spoilage | Cover produce, elevate from ground, increase ventilation |
| High Heat (>40°C) | Shelf life reduced 40% | Accelerated spoilage, insect infestation increases | Move to shade, consider selling quickly |
| Moderate Rain | Shelf life reduced 25% | Some moisture risk | Cover crops well, check daily |
| Cold/Winter | Shelf life INCREASED 20% | Frost damage possible for some crops | Good for storage — but protect from frost |
| Normal | Standard shelf life | Follow standard procedures | Regular monitoring every 15 days |

## Warehouse Options in India

### Government Warehouses
- **FCI (Food Corporation of India)**: Wheat, Rice — during procurement season
- **CWC (Central Warehousing Corporation)**: All commodities, rented space available
- **State Warehousing Corporations**: State-level storage, often subsidized rates

### Warehouse Receipt System
- Deposit your produce at approved warehouse
- Get a warehouse receipt (negotiable instrument)
- Can pledge receipt for bank loan (60-70% of value)
- Sell produce when prices are favorable without physically moving it

### How to Use Warehouse Receipt:
1. Take produce to nearest CWC/SWC warehouse
2. Get quality tested and graded
3. Receive electronic warehouse receipt
4. Use receipt as collateral for bank loan
5. Sell when prices improve — buyer picks up from warehouse
