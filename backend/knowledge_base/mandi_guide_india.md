# Complete Guide to APMC Mandis in India
## How Agricultural Markets Work — For Indian Farmers
## Last Updated: March 2026

---

## What is an APMC Mandi?

APMC stands for **Agricultural Produce Market Committee**. These are regulated wholesale markets (mandis) established under the Agricultural Produce Marketing (Regulation) Act enacted by each state. Every state has its own APMC Act.

APMCs were created to:
- Protect farmers from exploitation by middlemen
- Ensure fair weighing and transparent price discovery
- Provide market infrastructure (auction halls, cold storage, weighbridges)
- Collect market fees for infrastructure maintenance

### Key Facts:
- India has approximately 7,000+ APMC mandis and 22,000+ sub-mandis
- Every agricultural commodity must be sold through regulated markets (with some exceptions after 2020 reforms)
- Mandi prices are reported daily to Agmarknet (agmarknet.gov.in)

## How Selling at a Mandi Works (Step by Step)

### Step 1: Arrive at Mandi
- Bring your produce to the nearest APMC mandi
- You can check mandi timings — most mandis operate 6 AM to 6 PM
- Some mandis have specific days for specific commodities

### Step 2: Commission Agent (Adatiya/Dalal)
- At the mandi, you typically work through a licensed commission agent (arthiya/adatiya)
- The agent handles the auction process and finds buyers
- Commission is typically 1-2.5% of sale value (varies by state)
- You can also sell directly through e-NAM without a commission agent at some mandis

### Step 3: Weighing and Grading
- Your produce is weighed on calibrated weighbridges
- Quality is visually assessed (moisture, foreign matter, grain size)
- For e-NAM mandis, scientific assaying is done (moisture meter, grading)

### Step 4: Auction/Price Discovery
- **Open Auction**: Traders bid openly, highest bid wins
- **e-NAM Auction**: Online bidding — traders from ANY mandi can bid
- **Private Negotiation**: Direct deal with trader (common but less transparent)

### Step 5: Payment
- After sale, payment should be made within 24-72 hours
- APMC rules mandate prompt payment
- e-NAM ensures electronic payment to bank account
- Always insist on immediate payment or bank transfer

## Mandi Fee Structure (State-wise)

Fees charged at APMC mandis vary significantly by state. Here is the typical breakdown:

### Common Fee Components:
| Fee Type | Typical Range | Who Pays |
|----------|--------------|----------|
| Mandi Fee (Market Fee) | 0.5% - 2% of sale value | Buyer/Commission Agent |
| Commission (Dalali) | 1% - 2.5% | Farmer (deducted from sale) |
| Cess | 0.5% - 1% | Buyer |
| Weighing Charges | Rs 2-5 per bag | Farmer |
| Loading/Unloading | Rs 3-8 per bag | Farmer |

- e-NAM mandis typically have lower effective fees

## How Agmarknet Reports Prices

**Agmarknet** (Agricultural Marketing Information Network) is the government portal that reports daily mandi prices across India.

### What Agmarknet Shows:
- **Minimum Price**: Lowest price at which the commodity was sold that day
- **Maximum Price**: Highest price at which it was sold
- **Modal Price**: Most common price (majority of transactions happened at this price)

### How to Read Prices:
- **Modal Price** is the most reliable indicator — it's what most farmers actually got
- Maximum price may be for premium quality/special variety
- Minimum price may be for damaged/low-quality produce
- All prices are in **Rs per Quintal (100 kg)**

### Price Reporting Process:
1. Mandi secretary records all transactions during the day
2. Data is uploaded to Agmarknet by evening (usually by 9:30 PM IST)
3. Data becomes available at agmarknet.gov.in and data.gov.in API
4. MandiMitra fetches this data daily for analysis

### Important Notes:
- Not all mandis report every day — some report with 1-2 day delay
- Some small mandis don't report to Agmarknet at all
- Prices can vary significantly between mandis even in the same district
- Weekend/holiday data may be missing

## e-NAM (National Agriculture Market)

e-NAM is a pan-India electronic trading portal launched in 2016 that links APMC mandis across India into a unified national market.

### How e-NAM Works:
1. **Registration**: Register at any e-NAM enabled mandi (need Aadhaar + bank account)
2. **Lot Creation**: Bring produce to mandi → weighed and graded scientifically
3. **Online Bidding**: Your lot details are uploaded online → traders from any connected mandi can bid
4. **Price Discovery**: You get bids from multiple traders (not just local ones)
5. **Payment**: Electronic payment to your bank account within 24-72 hours

### Benefits of e-NAM:
- Access to buyers from other states (better price discovery)
- Scientific quality assaying (no subjective grading)
- Electronic payment (no cash, no delay)
- Price transparency (you can see all bids)
- Reduced commission agent dependency

### How to Register on e-NAM:
1. Visit your nearest e-NAM enabled mandi
2. Bring: Aadhaar card, bank passbook/cancelled cheque, passport photo
3. Fill registration form at e-NAM kiosk
4. Mandi secretary verifies details (1-7 days)
5. Receive login credentials via email/SMS
6. Download the e-NAM mobile app for price tracking

### e-NAM Coverage:
- 1,000+ mandis integrated across 23 states and 4 UTs
- States fully integrated: AP, Chhattisgarh, Gujarat, Haryana, HP, Jharkhand, Karnataka, Kerala, MP, Maharashtra, Odisha, Punjab, Rajasthan, Tamil Nadu, Telangana, UP, Uttarakhand, West Bengal

## Tips for Getting Better Prices at Mandi

### Before Going to Mandi:
1. **Check MandiMitra**: Know today's prices at nearby mandis before you go
2. **Compare MSP**: If mandi price < MSP, sell to government procurement center instead
3. **Grade your produce**: Sort by quality — sell premium separately from regular
4. **Time your visit**: Avoid peak arrival days (too much supply = lower prices)
5. **Clean and dry**: Remove foreign matter, dry properly — FAQ quality fetches 5-10% more

### At the Mandi:
1. **Don't accept first offer**: Wait for competitive bidding
2. **Know your costs**: Calculate your total cost including transport, labor, storage
3. **Get weighment slip**: Always take official weighment receipt
4. **Demand prompt payment**: APMC rules mandate payment within 24-72 hours
5. **Check deductions**: Verify that only legitimate fees are deducted

### Negotiation Strategies:
1. **Show price brief**: Use MandiMitra's negotiation brief showing MSP, nearby mandi prices, and trends
2. **Mention alternatives**: "I can send this to [another mandi] where price is higher"
3. **Quality premium**: If your produce is better quality, demand premium over modal price
4. **Bulk advantage**: Large quantities should command better unit price
5. **Timing**: Sell in morning when traders are actively buying, not at end of day

## Government Schemes for Farmers

### PM-KISAN (Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi)
- **Benefit**: Rs 6,000/year in 3 installments of Rs 2,000 each
- **Eligibility**: All farmer families with cultivable land
- **How to Apply**: Through CSC (Common Service Center), PM-KISAN portal, or state agriculture office
- **Registration**: Aadhaar + land records + bank account

### PMFBY (Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana)
- **Benefit**: Crop insurance against natural calamities
- **Premium**: 2% for Kharif, 1.5% for Rabi, 5% for horticulture
- **Coverage**: Comprehensive — drought, flood, hailstorm, pest attack
- **How to Apply**: Through bank (with crop loan) or CSC portal

### Warehouse Receipt Finance
- **Benefit**: Loan of 60-70% of crop value against warehouse receipt
- **Interest Rate**: 7-9% per annum
- **Use Case**: Store produce, get loan for expenses, sell when prices are better

### PM-AASHA (Annadata Aashirwad)
- **Benefit**: Government procures pulses, oilseeds at MSP when market price falls below MSP
- **How**: Through NAFED at designated procurement centers
- **Crops**: Pulses (Chana, Moong, Urad, Tur, Masur) and Oilseeds (Soyabean, Mustard, Groundnut)
