## What is MSP?

Minimum Support Price (MSP) is the price at which the Government of India purchases crops from farmers, regardless of market price. MSP acts as a safety net — if market prices fall below MSP, the government procures at MSP to protect farmers from losses.

MSP is announced by the CCEA based on recommendations from the Commission for Agricultural Costs and Prices (CACP). Since the Union Budget 2018-19, MSP is fixed at a minimum of 1.5 times the All-India weighted average cost of production (A2+FL cost).

### Who Procures at MSP?
- **Food Corporation of India (FCI)**: Wheat, Rice (Paddy)
- **NAFED**: Pulses (Chana, Moong, Urad, Tur), Oilseeds (Mustard, Groundnut, Soyabean)
- **Cotton Corporation of India (CCI)**: Cotton
- **State Agencies**: Vary by state — e.g., MP State Cooperative for Wheat in Madhya Pradesh

### How to Sell at MSP?
1. Register at your nearest APMC mandi or e-NAM portal
2. Bring produce to designated MSP procurement center during procurement season
3. Produce must meet quality standards (FAQ - Fair Average Quality)
4. Payment is made directly to your bank account within 72 hours

## Crops WITHOUT MSP

The following commonly traded crops do NOT have government MSP:
- **Onion** — No MSP. Prices are purely market-driven. Government intervenes via export bans/duties and buffer stock operations by NAFED.
- **Tomato** — No MSP. Highly perishable, market-driven pricing.
- **Potato** — No MSP. Cold storage and market timing are key.
- **Garlic** — No MSP.
- **Turmeric (Haldi)** — No MSP. Traded on NCDEX.
- **Red Chilli** — No MSP.
- **Coriander (Dhania)** — No MSP.
- **Cumin (Jeera)** — No MSP. Traded on NCDEX.

For these crops, farmers should rely on mandi prices, negotiate based on market trends, and consider storage/timing strategies.

## Important MSP Procurement Facts

- **Wheat procurement** happens primarily in April-May (Rabi harvest season)
- **Paddy procurement** happens in October-December (Kharif harvest season)
- **Pulse procurement** by NAFED happens at designated centers announced by state governments
- Farmers must have **Aadhaar, bank account, and land records** for MSP procurement
- MSP procurement requires produce to meet **FAQ (Fair Average Quality)** standards
- Payment is **direct bank transfer** — no middlemen

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What is the current MSP for Wheat?**
A: Rs 2,585/quintal for Rabi 2026-27 (latest). Previous season was Rs 2,425/quintal.

**Q: What is the current MSP for Soyabean?**
A: Rs 5,328/quintal for Kharif 2025-26. It increased by Rs 436 from Rs 4,892 in 2024-25.

**Q: Which crop got the highest MSP increase recently?**
A: For Kharif 2025-26, Nigerseed got the highest increase of Rs 820/quintal. For Rabi 2026-27, Safflower got the highest increase of Rs 600/quintal.

**Q: Does Onion have MSP?**
A: No. Onion, Tomato, Potato, Garlic, Turmeric, Chilli, Coriander, and Cumin do not have government MSP. Their prices are purely market-driven.

**Q: How do I sell at MSP?**
A: Register at your nearest APMC mandi or e-NAM portal, bring produce to MSP procurement center during procurement season, ensure FAQ quality, and receive payment in your bank account within 72 hours.
